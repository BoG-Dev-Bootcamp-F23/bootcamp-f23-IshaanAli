Georgia Institute of Technology

  • History

    Founded on Oct. 13, 1885, the Georgia School of Technology opened its doors in October 1888 to 84 students. The School’s creation signaled the beginning of the transformation of the agrarian South to an industrial economy. During its first 50 years, Tech grew from a narrowly focused trade school to a regionally recognized technological university. In 1948, the School’s name was changed to the Georgia Institute of Technology to reflect a growing focus on advanced technological and scientific research. In recent years, Georgia Tech has been a national leader in managing the global transition from an industrial economy to an information economy. Throughout its long history, Georgia Tech has always focused its efforts on preparing students to use their innovative skills and strong work ethic to solve real-world problems and improve the lives of people around the globe.

  • Academics

    Georgia Tech boasts impressive academic statistics and consistently ranks among the top institutions in various fields. In engineering and technology disciplines, Georgia Tech is renowned, consistently securing a top spot in national and global rankings. Its computer science and engineering programs, for example, are highly regarded, often placing within the top 10 in the United States. Furthermore, the Institute's commitment to research and innovation is evident through its robust publication output and contributions to cutting-edge advancements. Georgia Tech's dedication to academic excellence extends beyond engineering, as it also excels in business, architecture, and the sciences, making it a dynamic and highly respected institution in higher education.

  • Campus & Culture

    The Georgia Tech campus is a vibrant and diverse community situated in the heart of Atlanta. Its urban location provides students with access to a wealth of cultural and professional opportunities. The campus itself is a mix of modern and historic architecture, featuring cutting-edge research facilities and beautiful green spaces. Georgia Tech's culture is characterized by a strong sense of innovation and entrepreneurship, fostering an environment where students are encouraged to pursue their creative ideas and turn them into real-world solutions. The collaborative spirit on campus is palpable, with students and faculty working together on groundbreaking research projects and startups. Additionally, Georgia Tech's commitment to diversity and inclusion ensures that students from all backgrounds feel welcome and valued, contributing to a vibrant and inclusive campus culture.

Cozily nestled in the shadow of Atlanta’s skyline, Bobby Dodd Stadium at Hyundai Field is easily one of the nation’s most unique and historic settings for college football. The venerable facility — the oldest on-campus stadium in NCAA Division I FBS — opened in 1913 and 2023 will be its 111th season as the home of Georgia Tech football.
